Communication Etiquettes

Digital communication includes email, texting, instant messaging, phone calls, audio or video conferencing, and social networking. To maintain good communication etiquette, it is important to:

A) Be Precise

Respect time: Do not waste others' time unless the message is relevant, and avoid expecting instant responses.

Respect data limits: Avoid sending large attachments; use compressed files or cloud storage links instead.

B) Be Polite

Whether communication is real-time or delayed, politeness is essential. Avoid abusive language even in disagreement.

C) Be Credible

Be mindful when commenting or posting; building a credible online presence helps others trust your input.
